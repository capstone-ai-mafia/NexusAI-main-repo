## 1. Purpose

This manual sets out the human-resources policies that govern employment at Nexus Technologies. Its purpose is to:

- Give every employee a single, clear reference for how core people processes work — from working hours and leave to reviews, promotions, and offboarding.
- Translate the company's **Core Values** (Company Profile §4) into consistent, predictable practices, so people are treated fairly regardless of team, level, or office.
- Establish a shared baseline that applies company-wide, while allowing **Regional Addenda** to meet or exceed the requirements of local law.
- Support managers in applying policy consistently and reduce ambiguity in day-to-day decisions.

This manual reflects the values of **clarity over cleverness** and **default to openness**: it is written to be understood, and it is available to every employee.

This manual is not an employment contract and does not alter the at-will or statutory nature of employment where applicable. Nexus may update these policies; material changes are announced through the sanctioned channels described in §17 and recorded in Workday and the NexusDocs *Nexus Home* space.

## 2. Scope

This manual applies to **all employees** of Nexus Technologies, Inc. and its subsidiaries, across all six offices and all remote arrangements, unless a section states otherwise.

| Group | Coverage |
|---|---|
| **Full-time and part-time employees** | Fully covered, with pro-ration where noted (e.g., leave accrual for part-time staff). |
| **Fixed-term / contract employees** | Covered for conduct, security, and workplace-behaviour sections; benefit and leave eligibility as stated in their agreement. |
| **Interns and apprentices** | Covered for conduct, onboarding, and workplace-behaviour sections; leave and benefits per their program terms. |
| **Independent contractors / agencies / vendors** | **Not employees.** Governed by their commercial agreement, not this manual, though they must follow the Code of Conduct (§11) and security requirements while engaged. |

**Geographic scope.** The manual applies across the Americas, EMEA, and APAC regions. Where local employment law in any jurisdiction (US, Portugal, India) mandates a different or more generous standard, the applicable **Regional Addendum** prevails for that jurisdiction.

**Remote and hybrid scope.** Employees working under hybrid or fully remote arrangements are fully covered by this manual; the mechanics of *how* remote work operates are governed by the **Remote Work Policy** (cross-referenced in §4, §12, §13, §16, and §17).

## 3. Employee Categories

Nexus classifies employees to make eligibility for hours, leave, and benefits clear. Category is recorded in Workday and shown on the employment agreement.

### 3.1 By working time

| Category | Definition | Typical eligibility |
|---|---|---|
| **Full-time** | Standard full working week (see §4). | Full leave, benefits, and review cycle. |
| **Part-time** | A reduced, agreed weekly schedule below full-time. | Pro-rated leave and eligible benefits based on contracted hours. |

### 3.2 By employment term

| Category | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Permanent** | Ongoing employment with no fixed end date. |
| **Fixed-term** | Employment for a defined period or project, with an end date in the agreement. |
| **Intern / Apprentice** | Time-boxed developmental role under a structured program. |

### 3.3 Probationary period

New permanent employees serve a **probationary period of up to three (3) months** (adjusted by Regional Addendum where local law differs). During probation:

- The employee receives structured check-ins at 30, 60, and 90 days (aligned with onboarding, §16).
- Either party may end employment with the notice defined in the employment agreement or local law.
- The employee accrues leave from day one but should coordinate significant time off with their manager.

Successful completion is confirmed in writing by the reporting manager in Workday.

### 3.4 Employment relationship to the org structure

Every employee has **one functional reporting manager** (the reporting line) and, where applicable, **one delivery lead** through their Squad or Pod (the dotted delivery line), exactly as defined in the Company Profile (§8). HR matters — leave approval, reviews, promotions, and conduct — always run through the **functional reporting line**, never the delivery line.

## 4. Working Hours

Nexus operates a **hybrid-first model** (Company Profile §11). This section defines standard hours; the **Remote Work Policy** governs where and how those hours may be worked remotely.

### 4.1 Standard hours

- **Standard working week:** 40 hours, typically Monday–Friday. (Regional Addenda align the working week with local norms — for example, offices in Jordan may observe a Sunday–Thursday week; the applicable addendum governs.)
- **Core collaboration hours:** 10:00–15:00 in each employee's home-office time zone. Employees are expected to be reachable and available for synchronous collaboration during this window; the remaining hours are flexible around it.
- **Async-by-default:** Consistent with our **Clarity** value (Company Profile §4), decisions and updates are written down first, so distributed teammates across time zones are not blocked on live meetings.

### 4.2 Anchor days and in-office expectations

- Office-based employees are expected on-site **three days per week**, with **Tuesday–Thursday** as the standard **Core Collaboration Days** (anchor days).
- **Wednesday** is a protected company-wide **focus block** — no internal recurring meetings — to preserve deep-work time.
- Exceptions, fully remote roles, and location-based variations are governed by the **Remote Work Policy**, not this manual.

### 4.3 Overtime and time recording

- Nexus is an outcomes-driven organization; most professional roles are not measured by clocked hours.
- For roles that are eligible for overtime under local law (recorded in the Regional Addendum), overtime must be approved in advance by the reporting manager and logged in Workday.
- **On-call:** Engineering on-call rotations (PagerDuty) are compensated or time-offset per the Regional Addendum and the Engineering on-call standard. Emergency incident work outside normal hours is followed by adequate rest before the next scheduled shift.

### 4.4 Rest, breaks, and disconnection

- Employees are entitled to reasonable daily breaks and a proper lunch period.
- Nexus supports a healthy **right to disconnect**: outside core hours, employees are not expected to respond to non-urgent messages. Urgent items follow the incident/escalation channels defined in the Company Profile (§12), not general chat.

## 5. Annual Leave

### 5.1 Company baseline entitlement

Nexus provides paid annual leave on a tenure-based scale. The figures below are the **company baseline**; Regional Addenda **meet or exceed** local statutory minimums, and where local law grants more, the higher figure applies.

| Completed years of service | Annual leave (full-time) |
|---|---|
| 0 – 2 years | 20 working days |
| 3 – 5 years | 23 working days |
| 6+ years | 25 working days |

- Part-time employees receive **pro-rated** leave based on contracted hours.
- Entitlement is **in addition to** public holidays (§8) and sick leave (§6).

### 5.2 Accrual, carryover, and payout

- Leave **accrues monthly** across the calendar year and is tracked in Workday.
- Employees may **carry over up to 5 unused days** into the next year; carried-over days must be used within the first quarter (by 31 March) or they lapse, unless local law requires otherwise.
- On leaving the company, accrued-but-unused statutory leave is paid out per local law and the Regional Addendum (see §18, Offboarding).

### 5.3 Requesting leave

- Requests are submitted in **Workday** and approved by the reporting manager.
- Submit routine requests with reasonable notice: **at least 2 weeks** for 3+ consecutive days, longer for extended leave.
- Managers approve based on team coverage and the delivery calendar. Where a request is declined for coverage reasons, the manager proposes an alternative window.
- **Squad coverage:** before extended leave, the employee confirms a **DRI handoff** so no work item is left without an owner (Company Profile §7.2).

### 5.4 Remote and location considerations

Working remotely from a location other than the employee's registered work location is **not** annual leave, but it may have tax, security, and legal implications. Such "work-from-anywhere" arrangements are governed by the **Remote Work Policy**, not by taking annual leave.

## 6. Sick Leave

### 6.1 Entitlement

- Nexus provides **paid sick leave** for genuine illness or injury, with a company baseline of **up to 10 paid sick days per year**, separate from annual leave. Regional Addenda adjust this to meet or exceed local statutory sick-pay requirements.
- Sick leave is **not** to be used as additional annual leave and does not accrue for payout.

### 6.2 Notification and documentation

- Notify your manager **as early as possible** on the first day of absence — ideally before core hours — via NexusConnect or the method agreed with your manager, and log the absence in Workday.
- For absences of **3 or more consecutive working days**, a medical certificate may be required (per Regional Addendum).
- For longer or recurring illness, the People team coordinates with the employee on medical leave, accommodations, and any statutory long-term sick provisions.

### 6.3 Working while unwell / remote sick days

- Employees who are unwell should rest and recover, not work through illness. A sick day is a sick day, whether the employee is office-based or remote.
- Nexus does **not** expect remote employees to "work from home because they're only a little sick." If you are unfit to work, record sick leave. Home-working arrangements otherwise follow the **Remote Work Policy**.

### 6.4 Related leave types

- **Mental health & wellbeing:** sick leave explicitly covers mental-health needs. Confidential support is available through the Employee Assistance Program (§14).
- **Caregiver / family emergency leave:** short-notice leave to care for a dependent is available per the Regional Addendum; coordinate with your manager and People.
- **Bereavement leave:** paid compassionate leave is provided for the death of a close family member (duration per Regional Addendum).

## 7. Maternity, Paternity & Parental Leave

Nexus supports employees becoming parents through birth, adoption, or surrogacy. The company uses a **caregiver-based framework** so entitlements are inclusive of all parents, alongside statutory maternity/paternity provisions.

### 7.1 Company baseline

| Role | Company baseline paid leave |
|---|---|
| **Primary caregiver** (the parent taking main responsibility after birth/placement) | **16 weeks** fully paid |
| **Secondary caregiver** | **6 weeks** fully paid |

- These are **company baselines**. Where local statutory maternity, paternity, or parental leave is **more generous**, the statutory entitlement applies — employees receive the **better** of the two, never less.
- Adoption and surrogacy are treated the same as birth for the purposes of caregiver leave.
- Leave may be taken flexibly within the window allowed by the Regional Addendum (e.g., a phased return).

### 7.2 Continuity and return

- **Job protection:** an employee returning from parental leave returns to the same role, or a comparable one at the same level and pay.
- **Benefits continuity:** benefits (§14) continue during paid parental leave.
- **Reviews and promotion:** time on parental leave does not count against an employee in performance reviews (§9) or promotion timing (§10). Goals and cycles are adjusted pro-rata.
- **Phased return / flexibility:** returning parents may arrange a temporary adjusted or remote schedule. Any remote component is governed by the **Remote Work Policy**.

### 7.3 Related provisions

- **Pregnancy accommodations** (e.g., adjusted duties, additional breaks) are provided as needed.
- **Pregnancy loss:** compassionate paid leave is available; coordinate confidentially with People.
- **Fertility, family-forming, and lactation support** are described in the Benefits section (§14) and Regional Addenda.

## 8. Public Holidays

- Public holidays are observed **according to each employee's office location and legal entity**, not a single global calendar. An employee in Austin, Denver, Lisbon, or Bengaluru observes the holidays of that location.
- The official holiday calendar for each office is published annually in Workday and the NexusDocs *Nexus Home* space.
- Public holidays are **paid** and are **separate** from annual leave (§5).

### 8.1 Working on a public holiday

- Employees are not normally required to work on a public holiday. Where operational coverage (e.g., on-call, customer-critical support) requires it, the employee receives **time off in lieu** or holiday pay per the Regional Addendum.

### 8.2 Cross-region collaboration

- Because holidays differ by office, teammates should check the shared holiday calendar before scheduling cross-office deadlines or meetings. This is an extension of the async-by-default norm (§4.1): don't assume everyone is working the same days.

### 8.3 Floating / flexible holidays

- In addition to fixed public holidays, employees receive **2 floating personal days** per year to observe a religious, cultural, or personal occasion of their choosing. Floating days are requested in Workday like annual leave and do not carry over.

## 9. Performance Reviews

Performance management at Nexus is continuous, values-based, and consistent across both career tracks.

### 9.1 Framework

- Reviews assess **what** an employee delivered (impact and outcomes) and **how** they delivered it (alignment with the five Core Values, Company Profile §4).
- The **L1–L7 leveling framework** (Company Profile §7) defines the expectations at each level, applied equally to the **IC track** and the **Management track**.
- Reviews run through the **functional reporting manager**, with structured input from the employee's **Squad/Pod delivery lead** and peers.

### 9.2 Cycle

| Event | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal-setting** | Start of each half | Employee and manager agree on outcomes tied to team/Squad objectives. |
| **Ongoing 1:1s** | Weekly / biweekly | Continuous feedback, coaching, and course-correction — no surprises at review time. |
| **Mid-year check-in** | Mid fiscal year | Lightweight progress review and goal adjustment. |
| **Annual review** | End of fiscal year | Formal written review, rating, and development plan. |

Reviews are aligned to the **Quarterly Business Review (QBR)** rhythm at the pillar level (Company Profile §13.4) so individual goals ladder up to company goals.

### 9.3 Ratings and calibration

- Reviews produce a **rating** on a defined scale (e.g., *Below Expectations → Meets → Exceeds → Outstanding*), covering both impact and values.
- Ratings are **calibrated** across each department by managers and Directors to ensure consistency and reduce individual-manager bias. People (HR) facilitates calibration.
- Ratings inform compensation, development planning, and promotion readiness (§10).

### 9.4 Feedback principles

- Feedback is **direct but respectful** (Core Value 6 and Company Profile §8 professional-behaviour standards): specific, actionable, and focused on the work, not the person.
- **360° input** (peers, delivery leads, and where relevant, cross-functional partners) is gathered for senior levels.

### 9.5 Underperformance

- Underperformance is addressed early and supportively through a documented **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** with clear objectives, support, and a defined review period.
- A PIP is a genuine opportunity to succeed, not a formality. People (HR) partners with the manager throughout. Outcomes and any consequences follow the Regional Addendum and local law.

### 9.6 Remote employees

- Remote and hybrid employees are reviewed on the **same standards and outcomes** as office-based peers. Physical presence is never a performance criterion; the **Remote Work Policy** reinforces that evaluation is outcome-based.

## 10. Promotions

Promotions recognize sustained performance at the **next** level — not merely tenure or strong performance at the current level.

### 10.1 Principles

- **Track-neutral:** the IC track and Management track are treated as equal in seniority and compensation at each level (Company Profile §7). Promotion on the IC track is valued as highly as moving into management.
- **Evidence-based:** promotion is based on demonstrated, sustained impact at the target level against the L1–L7 expectations — shown, not asserted.
- **No self-approval:** consistent with the company's approval principle (Company Profile §9), no manager promotes their own report unilaterally; promotions go through a review panel.

### 10.2 Process

1. **Nomination.** The reporting manager (the sponsor) nominates the employee, or an employee raises interest with their manager, during a promotion cycle.
2. **Promotion packet.** The manager and employee assemble evidence: impact examples, peer/stakeholder input, and mapping to the target level's expectations.
3. **Calibration panel.** A cross-team panel of managers and Directors reviews packets together to ensure a consistent bar across the department.
4. **Approval.** Promotions follow the approval hierarchy consistent with the Company Profile (§9.2): Director and VP sign-off, with People (HR) validating leveling and compensation; VP-and-above promotions escalate per that framework.
5. **Communication.** Approved promotions are recorded in Workday, reflected in compensation, and announced through the appropriate channel.

### 10.3 Cadence

- Promotion cycles run **twice per fiscal year**, aligned with the review calendar (§9). Off-cycle promotions are possible only in exceptional cases (e.g., a role scope change) and require the same approvals.

### 10.4 Fairness safeguards

- Calibration panels and People (HR) monitor promotion outcomes for consistency and equity across demographics, offices, and remote vs. office-based employees.
- Time on protected leave (e.g., parental leave, §7) never disadvantages promotion timing.

## 11. Code of Conduct

This section summarizes the standard; the full detail lives in the **Code of Business Conduct & Ethics**. All employees, contractors, and interns are bound by it.

### 11.1 Foundations

Conduct at Nexus is grounded in the Core Values (Company Profile §4), especially **Ownership**, **Integrity**, and **Clarity**. Employees are expected to:

- Act with **honesty and integrity** in all dealings, internally and with customers, partners, and regulators.
- **Comply with the law** in every jurisdiction where Nexus operates.
- **Own their decisions** and raise concerns early rather than concealing problems.

### 11.2 Core obligations

| Area | Expectation |
|---|---|
| **Conflicts of interest** | Disclose any personal, financial, or family interest that could conflict with your role or Nexus's interests. When in doubt, disclose. |
| **Confidentiality** | Protect Nexus, customer, and colleague information. Confidential and customer data may only be accessed on a need-to-know basis. |
| **Data protection & privacy** | Handle personal data lawfully and per the privacy standards owned by Legal & Compliance. |
| **Security** | Follow the security requirements owned by the CISO/InfoSec team — device security, access controls, phishing vigilance, and incident reporting. |
| **Anti-bribery & corruption** | Never offer or accept bribes, kickbacks, or improper gifts. Modest, customary business hospitality is permitted within the gifts standard. |
| **Fair dealing** | Compete fairly and honestly; never misrepresent Nexus's products or a competitor's. |
| **Company assets** | Use company systems, devices, and funds responsibly and primarily for legitimate business purposes. |
| **Accurate records** | Keep truthful business, financial, and time records; never falsify data — including demo or evaluation data presented to customers. |

### 11.3 Confidentiality and remote work

- Confidentiality obligations apply **identically** whether working in an office or remotely. Remote employees must protect screens, conversations, and documents from household or public exposure. The specific home-office security requirements (secure networks, device handling, screen privacy) are detailed in the **Remote Work Policy** and the InfoSec standards.

### 11.4 Reporting concerns

- Employees can raise conduct or ethics concerns to their manager, People (HR), Legal & Compliance, or through a **confidential reporting channel**.
- Nexus prohibits **retaliation** against anyone who raises a concern in good faith (§13.4).

## 12. Dress Code

Nexus keeps dress code simple and respectful, consistent with **clarity over cleverness** — we care far more about the work than the wardrobe.

### 12.1 General standard

- The default is **business casual**. Employees should be clean, presentable, and comfortable.
- Teams may set a more casual norm for internal, non-customer days, at the manager's discretion.

### 12.2 Customer-facing and event days

- When meeting customers on-site, presenting at events, or representing Nexus externally, employees dress **one level up** — **smart business** or as the occasion and customer context require. Sales, Customer Success, and Professional Services teams calibrate to the client.

### 12.3 Remote video etiquette

- On video calls (**NexusMeet**), remote and hybrid employees follow the **same business-casual standard** as in-office for customer-facing or formal meetings. For internal calls, present a professional appearance and background.
- Remote-specific expectations for background, environment, and virtual meeting conduct are set out in the **Remote Work Policy**.

### 12.4 Inclusivity

- The dress code is inclusive of religious, cultural, and personal expression, and of reasonable accommodations. It is applied consistently and never used to single out any individual or group.

## 13. Workplace Behaviour

Nexus is committed to a safe, respectful, inclusive workplace — in every office and in every remote channel.

### 13.1 Respect and professional communication

- Interactions are **direct but respectful** (Company Profile §8). Dismissive or personal language is not acceptable — feedback targets the work, not the person. ("This is wrong" becomes "this breaks on empty input — here's why.")
- This standard applies **everywhere**: in person, in NexusConnect, in code reviews, in NexusMeet calls, and in documents.

### 13.2 Anti-harassment and anti-discrimination

- Nexus has **zero tolerance** for harassment, bullying, or discrimination based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, age, disability, marital or family status, or any other characteristic protected by local law.
- This covers all forms — verbal, physical, visual, and digital — and applies to conduct at work, at work events, on business travel, and in company digital spaces.

### 13.3 Diversity, equity & inclusion

- Consistent with our **Curiosity** value (Company Profile §4), Nexus fosters an inclusive environment where diverse perspectives are actively welcomed. Inclusion is expected of everyone, not delegated to a committee.
- Distributed and remote employees are deliberately included in decisions, information flow, and social connection so that being outside HQ is never a disadvantage — an inclusion principle reinforced by the **Remote Work Policy**.

### 13.4 Reporting, investigation, and non-retaliation

- Concerns about behaviour can be raised with a manager, People (HR), or the confidential reporting channel.
- Reports are handled **promptly, fairly, and as confidentially as possible**. Investigations follow a consistent process, and both the person raising a concern and the person named are treated fairly.
- **Retaliation** against anyone who reports a concern or participates in an investigation in good faith is itself a serious violation and is prohibited.

### 13.5 Substance, safety, and workplace conduct

- Employees must not be impaired by alcohol or unlawful substances while working or representing Nexus. Reasonable hospitality at company events is fine in moderation.
- Everyone is responsible for a safe workplace and for reporting safety hazards (in-office) or ergonomic issues (remote — see the Remote Work Policy for home-office setup support).

## 15. Training & Development

## 16. Onboarding

Onboarding gives new employees the tools, context, and relationships to become effective quickly — whether they start in an office or remotely.

### 16.1 Before day one (pre-boarding)

- People (HR) confirms the offer, paperwork, and start details in Workday.
- **IT & Corporate Systems** provisions accounts and equipment. Access to the sanctioned tools — NexusConnect, NexusDocs, NexusBoard, NexusMeet, and Workday (plus GitHub for source control, as relevant) — is set up per role, following least-privilege access owned by IT and Security.
- For remote or hybrid starters, equipment shipping and home-office setup follow the **Remote Work Policy**.

### 16.2 First week

- **Welcome & orientation:** company overview (grounded in the Company Profile), values, structure, and how work flows through Squads and DRIs.
- **Manager kickoff:** the reporting manager sets initial expectations and introduces the team and the employee's Squad/Pod.
- **Onboarding buddy:** a peer is assigned to answer everyday questions and help the new joiner navigate norms and channels.
- **Mandatory training:** security-awareness and compliance training (§15.2) are completed in the first week.

### 16.3 The 30–60–90 plan

| Milestone | Focus |
|---|---|
| **First 30 days** | Learn the systems, tools, team, and product surfaces; complete required training; make first small contributions. |
| **First 60 days** | Take ownership of defined tasks; understand how the role connects to the Squad and to customers. |
| **First 90 days** | Operate independently at level; a check-in confirms probation status (§3.3) and sets forward goals. |

### 16.4 Remote onboarding

- Remote onboarding follows the **same milestones**, delivered virtually. Extra care is taken to include remote joiners in team rituals and informal connection. The mechanics (equipment, secure access, virtual first-day logistics) are governed by the **Remote Work Policy**.

## 17. Offboarding

Offboarding is handled respectfully, securely, and consistently — whether the departure is voluntary or involuntary.

### 17.1 Notice

- **Resignation:** employees give the notice stated in their employment agreement or local law (commonly a minimum notice period; managerial roles may require more). Notice is submitted in writing and recorded in Workday.
- **Involuntary exit:** handled with dignity, documented properly, and in line with the Regional Addendum and local law, with People (HR) involved throughout.

### 17.2 Transition and handover

- Before departure, the leaving employee completes a **DRI handover** (Company Profile §7.2): every owned work item, document, and responsibility is reassigned so nothing is left without an owner.
- Knowledge is documented in NexusDocs per the anti-silo principle.

### 17.3 Access, assets, and security

- On the last working day, **IT and Security** revoke system access following least-privilege deprovisioning. Access is removed promptly to protect company and customer data.
- Company equipment is returned. For remote employees, equipment return logistics follow the **Remote Work Policy**.
- Confidentiality, IP, and any post-employment obligations from the employment agreement **continue after departure**.

### 17.4 Final pay and benefits

- Final salary, accrued-but-unused statutory leave (§5.2), and any other amounts owed are settled per local law and the Regional Addendum.
- Information on benefit continuation, equity treatment, and pension/retirement handling is provided by People (HR).

### 17.5 Exit interview

- Departing employees are offered a **confidential exit interview** with People (HR). Feedback is aggregated to improve the employee experience and is never used to disadvantage the individual.

### 17.6 Rehire

- Employees who leave in good standing are welcome to be considered for rehire. Records are retained per the data-retention standard owned by Legal & Compliance.

## 18. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. Do I have to be in the office on Core Collaboration Days (anchor days)?**
Office-based employees are expected on-site three days a week, with Tuesday–Thursday as Core Collaboration Days/anchor days (§4.2). Wednesday is a company-wide focus block. Fully remote roles and exceptions are governed by the **Remote Work Policy**.

**Q2. How do I request annual leave?**
Submit the request in Workday for your manager's approval, with at least two weeks' notice for 3+ consecutive days. Before extended leave, arrange a DRI handoff so your work has cover (§5.3).

**Q3. Can I carry unused annual leave into next year?**
Up to 5 days, and they must be used by 31 March or they lapse — unless your Regional Addendum grants more (§5.2).

**Q4. What do I do on my first sick day?**
Tell your manager as early as possible (ideally before core hours) and log it in Workday. A medical certificate may be needed for absences of 3+ consecutive days (§6.2).

**Q5. Which public holidays do I get?**
The ones observed at your office/legal entity — Austin, Denver, Lisbon, or Bengaluru. The calendar is in Workday. You also get 2 floating personal days (§8).

**Q6. How much parental leave will I get?**
The company baseline is 16 weeks (primary caregiver) or 6 weeks (secondary caregiver), fully paid — or your local statutory entitlement if it is more generous. You always receive the better of the two (§7.1).

**Q7. Is the IC track really equal to management for promotions?**
Yes. The tracks are equivalent at each level, and IC-track promotions are valued as highly as moving into management (§10.1).

**Q8. Are remote employees reviewed differently?**
No. Reviews are outcome-based, using the same standards for everyone. Physical presence is never a performance criterion (§9.6).

**Q9. Where do I report a conduct or harassment concern?**
To your manager, People (HR), Legal & Compliance, or the confidential reporting channel. Good-faith reports are protected from retaliation (§11.4, §13.4).

**Q10. What's the dress code for a customer meeting?**
Dress one level up from business casual — smart business — calibrated to the customer. On video, the same standard applies for customer-facing calls (§12).

**Q11. I'm starting remotely — how do I get my equipment and accounts?**
IT provisions accounts and ships equipment before day one; the specifics are in the **Remote Work Policy**. Your onboarding buddy and manager help you settle in (§16).

**Q12. What happens to my access and equipment when I leave?**
IT and Security revoke access on your last day, and equipment is returned (remote returns follow the Remote Work Policy). Final pay and any accrued statutory leave are settled per local law (§17).

**Q13. Where does this manual not apply?**
Where a Regional Addendum or local law requires a different or more generous standard — in that case, the local rule governs for that jurisdiction (§2).

**Q14. This manual and the Remote Work Policy seem to overlap — which wins?**
For remote/hybrid mechanics (eligibility, equipment, home-office security, location rules), the **Remote Work Policy** is authoritative. For everything else — leave, reviews, conduct, benefits — this manual governs. They are designed to work together.

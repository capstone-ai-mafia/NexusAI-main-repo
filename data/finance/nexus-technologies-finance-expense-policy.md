## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy governs how employees incur, submit, approve, and get reimbursed for business expenses, and how the company procures goods and services from vendors. It applies globally; regional variations (currency, tax documentation, per diem rates) are maintained as addenda by local Finance leads in Austin, Lisbon, and Bengaluru.

All expense, travel, and purchasing activity is administered through **Workday** (expense reporting, procurement requests, vendor master data) and the **NexusTravel Portal** (travel booking), both owned by Finance & Accounting.

## 2. Expense Reimbursement

### 2.1 Eligible Expenses
- Client meals, business meals with colleagues (max 1x/week per team unless traveling)
- Ground transportation, parking, tolls for business purposes
- Business travel costs not covered by corporate card (see Section 3)
- Professional development: conferences, certifications, courses (requires Manager pre-approval)
- Mileage for personal vehicle use on company business

### 2.2 Non-Reimbursable Expenses
- Personal entertainment, in-room movies, spa services
- Traffic or parking fines, toll violations
- Airline seat upgrades beyond policy class (Section 3.2)
- Spouse, partner, or family travel costs
- Personal subscriptions or software not approved by IT
- Alcohol outside reasonable client entertainment (see FAQ)

### 2.3 Receipts & Documentation
- Itemized receipts are **required for any expense over $25 USD**.
- Credit card statements alone are not sufficient documentation — the itemized receipt is required.
- Missing-receipt affidavits are permitted no more than twice per employee per fiscal year.

### 2.4 Submission Deadlines
- Expenses must be submitted in Workday within **30 calendar days** of being incurred.
- Expenses submitted more than **90 days** after the date incurred require Director approval and may be denied at Finance's discretion.

### 2.4a Expense Reimbursement Approval

Per Company Profile §9.3: expense reports **under $500** route to the Direct Manager only; expense reports **above $500** additionally route to Finance for validation before disbursement (see §9.1 Approval Workflow).

### 2.5 Reimbursement Timeline
- Approved expense reports are disbursed via direct deposit within **5–7 business days** of final approval.
- Mileage is reimbursed at the current IRS standard mileage rate, updated each January 1 and posted on NexusHub.

## 3. Business Travel

### 3.1 Booking
- All travel must be booked through the **NexusTravel Portal**, which auto-applies cost-center coding and negotiated corporate rates.
- Booking outside the portal requires advance Manager approval and may be reimbursed only up to the portal's equivalent fare/rate.
- Flights should be booked at least **14 days in advance** where the trip is known that far ahead.

### 3.2 Airfare Class Policy

| Nexus Level | Domestic | International (flight > 6 hrs) |
|---|---|---|
| L1–L4 (IC/Manager) | Economy | Economy / Premium Economy |
| L5–L6 (Staff–Director) | Economy | Premium Economy |
| L7 / ELT | Economy or Premium Economy | Business |

### 3.3 Hotel Guidelines

| City Tier | Example Cities | Nightly Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Austin, San Francisco, New York, Dublin, London, Singapore | $350 |
| Tier 2 | All other cities | $250 |

Exceptions (e.g., conference host hotels) require Director approval in advance.

### 3.4 Ground Transportation & Meals
- Ride-share, taxi, and rental car are reimbursable at coach/economy tier.
- Meal per diem: **$75/day domestic**, **$100/day international** (itemized receipts still encouraged but not required under per diem).

### 3.5 International & High-Risk Travel
- All international travel requires **Director approval**, regardless of cost.
- Travel to destinations flagged as elevated-risk requires notification to **IT & Security (GRC team)** at least 5 business days prior to departure.
- Company-booked travel includes automatic travel insurance coverage.

## 4. Corporate Credit Cards

### 4.1 Eligibility
Cards are issued to employees at **L3 and above**, or to any employee with recurring business expense needs (e.g., Sales, Customer Success, Procurement), upon Manager and Finance approval.

### 4.2 Monthly Limits

| Level | Monthly Limit |
|---|---|
| L1–L3 | $2,500 |
| L4–L5 | $5,000 |
| Director / VP | $10,000 |
| ELT | $25,000 |

Virtual, single-use cards are available through Procurement for one-off vendor purchases without a standing card.

### 4.3 Reconciliation
- Cardholders must code and attach receipts to every transaction in Workday within **5 business days** of the statement closing.
- Managers must approve reconciled statements within 3 business days of submission.

### 4.4 Lost, Stolen, or Compromised Cards
- Report immediately to **Finance & Accounting** and **IT & Security**.
- The card will be frozen within 1 business day and a replacement issued.

### 4.5 Prohibited Uses
Cash advances, personal purchases, personal gift cards, and any purchase outside approved business categories are prohibited. Repeated late reconciliation or misuse may result in card suspension and, for material or repeated violations, escalation to HR.

## 5. Purchase Requests

### 5.1 When a Purchase Request (PR) Is Required
Any purchase not made via corporate card — software subscriptions, hardware, contracted services, recurring vendor spend — requires a **Procurement Request Form (PRF)** submitted in Workday **before** the purchase is made.

### 5.2 Required Documentation
- Business justification
- Vendor quote or invoice
- Cost center / department code
- Confirmation of available budget (from budget owner — see Section 6)

### 5.3 Review SLA
- Standard PRs: reviewed within **3 business days**.
- Expedited PRs (requires VP sign-off to invoke): reviewed within **1 business day**.

### 5.4 Approval Routing
PRs route automatically based on total contract/purchase value, per the thresholds in Section 8 (Procurement Approval). Approval must be granted **before** a PO is issued or payment is committed.

## 6. Budget Limits

### 6.1 Budget Ownership
- Departmental annual operating budgets are approved by the **CFO** and the relevant **department VP** during Q4 annual planning for the following fiscal year.
- **Directors and VPs** own and are accountable for their org's budget lines; Managers submit spend requests against their Director's or VP's approved budget.

### 6.2 Expense & Purchase Approval Thresholds

*(Consistent with Company Profile Section 9.3)*

| Amount | Required Approver |
|---|---|
| Up to $1,000 | Direct Manager |
| $1,001 – $10,000 | Director |
| $10,001 – $50,000 | VP + CFO |
| $50,001 – $250,000 | CFO + relevant C-Suite sponsor |
| Above $250,000 | CFO + CEO |

*(Every tier above $10,000 includes the CFO, consistent with Company Profile §9.3.)*

### 6.3 Budget Reviews
- **FP&A** conducts quarterly budget reviews with each department.
- Department heads must present a variance explanation for any line item over/under budget by more than **10%**.

### 6.4 Unbudgeted / Exception Spend
Spend not in the approved annual budget requires written justification and sign-off **one level above** the standard threshold table (e.g., a $5,000 unbudgeted item requires Director approval instead of Manager).

## 7. Vendor Payments

### 7.1 New Vendor Onboarding
- New vendors must submit a completed **W-9** (US) or **W-8BEN** (international), banking details, and any required compliance documentation.
- Accounts Payable (AP) adds the vendor to the Workday vendor master before any invoice can be paid.

### 7.2 Payment Terms
- Standard terms: **Net 30** from invoice date, unless a different term is negotiated and documented in the vendor contract.

### 7.3 Payment Methods
- **ACH** is the preferred method for domestic vendors.
- **Wire transfer** for international vendors.
- **Check** issued only by exception, with Controller approval.

### 7.4 Invoice Submission & Matching
- Vendors submit invoices referencing the applicable PO number to Accounts Payable.
- All invoices undergo **3-way match** (Purchase Order, Receipt/Confirmation of Delivery, Invoice) before payment is released.
- Discrepancies are routed to Procurement for resolution before payment proceeds.

### 7.5 Payment Run Cadence
AP processes standard payment runs twice monthly — on the **15th** and the **last business day** of the month.

## 8. Procurement Approval

### 8.1 Contract & Vendor Approval Thresholds

*(Consistent with Company Profile Section 9.3, extended with additional value bands)*

| Contract Value | Required Sign-off Chain |
|---|---|
| Up to $25,000 | Department Director → Legal review |
| $25,001 – $100,000 | Department VP → Legal → Finance (Controller) |
| $100,001 – $500,000 | ELT sponsor → General Counsel → CFO |
| Above $500,000 | CEO + CFO + General Counsel |

### 8.2 Competitive Bidding
Purchases above **$50,000** require a minimum of **two vendor quotes**, unless a sole-source justification is submitted and approved.

### 8.3 Sole-Source Justification
Requires a written explanation of why only one vendor can meet the need, approved jointly by the requesting **Department VP** and the **Procurement Lead**.

### 8.4 Preferred Vendors
Procurement maintains a preferred vendor list for recurring spend categories (software, travel, contractors). Use of a non-preferred vendor in these categories requires Procurement sign-off in addition to the standard approval chain.

## 9. Approval Workflow

### 9.1 Expense Reimbursement Flow
```
Employee submits expense report (Workday)
        │
Routes to Direct Manager (or higher, per Section 6.2 threshold)
        │
Finance validates receipts & coding
        │
Accounts Payable disburses via direct deposit
```

### 9.2 Purchase Request Flow
```
Employee submits PRF (Workday) — before purchase
        │
Routes per Section 8 thresholds (Director / VP / ELT+Legal / CEO+CFO+GC)
        │
Procurement issues Purchase Order
        │
Vendor delivers goods/services & invoices
        │
AP performs 3-way match
        │
Payment released per Section 7 terms
```

### 9.3 Corporate Card Flow
```
Transaction occurs on corporate card
        │
Cardholder codes & attaches receipt in Workday (within 5 business days)
        │
Manager approves reconciliation (within 3 business days)
        │
Finance closes the statement period
```

### 9.4 Vendor Contract Flow
```
Requestor drafts Statement of Work / contract
        │
Legal review
        │
Approval chain per Section 8.1
        │
Procurement issues PO
        │
AP applies standard payment terms (Section 7)
```

## 10. FAQ

**Q: I lost a receipt — can I still get reimbursed?**
Yes, up to twice per fiscal year using a missing-receipt affidavit in Workday. Beyond that, Manager approval is required on a case-by-case basis.

**Q: Can I book travel outside the NexusTravel Portal?**
Only with advance Manager approval, and reimbursement is capped at what the portal would have cost for an equivalent booking.

**Q: How long does reimbursement take once approved?**
5–7 business days via direct deposit.

**Q: What happens if I exceed my corporate card limit?**
Transactions will decline. Contact Finance & Accounting to request a temporary limit increase before traveling or making a large purchase — this should be requested at least 3 business days in advance.

**Q: Who approves a purchase that wasn't in our department's budget?**
One level higher than the standard threshold in Section 6.2 — for example, a $3,000 unbudgeted item needs Director approval instead of Manager approval.

**Q: Can I expense alcohol?**
Reasonable alcohol as part of client entertainment is reimbursable. Alcohol for internal team events should go through your Director for approval rather than individual expense reports.

**Q: A vendor's invoice doesn't match our PO — what happens?**
It's held by Accounts Payable and routed to Procurement for resolution; payment is not released until the 3-way match is resolved.

**Q: Who do I contact with questions about this policy?**
Your local Finance & Accounting business partner, or the `#team-finance` Slack channel for general questions.

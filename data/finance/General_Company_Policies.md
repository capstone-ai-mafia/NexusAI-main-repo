## 1. Remote Work Policy

**Applicability:** Employees designated as "Remote-First" per the Company Profile's Working Model.

1.1 Remote-first employees must maintain a stable internet connection sufficient for **NexusMeet** video conferencing and **NexusConnect** availability during their designated Core Hours (10:00 AM – 3:00 PM local time).

1.2 Employees must attend the quarterly **Nexus Sync** in-person gathering at their nearest regional office unless excused by their Direct Manager and HR.

1.3 Home office equipment (laptop, monitor, peripherals) is provisioned through **IT Policy** procurement workflows; requests are submitted via the NexusHR Portal and routed to IT for fulfillment.

1.4 Remote employees are responsible for securing their home network in accordance with **Security Policy** minimum standards (see Security Policy §Endpoint & Network Security). Use of public/unsecured Wi-Fi for company work is prohibited without an active VPN connection.

1.5 Time-zone overlap with the employee's Pod or department must be maintained for at least the Core Hours window; exceptions require Program Manager and Direct Manager sign-off.

1.6 All remote work expenses (internet stipend, equipment) follow the reimbursement thresholds defined in the **Finance Policy / Approval Hierarchy**.

---

## 2. Hybrid Work Policy

**Applicability:** Employees designated as "Hybrid" per the Company Profile.

2.1 Hybrid employees are required in-office on **Core Collaboration Days (Tuesday–Thursday)** at their assigned office location, unless otherwise approved by their Direct Manager.

2.2 Monday and Friday are flexible remote days by default; teams may adjust this locally with Director approval, provided Core Collaboration Days remain fixed.

2.3 Office access (badge, desk booking) is managed through Operations; lost badges or access issues are reported to IT Support per **IT Policy** procedures.

2.4 Hybrid employees relocating outside a reasonable commuting distance of their assigned office must notify HR and their Manager in advance; continued hybrid status is not guaranteed and may convert to Remote-First pending role and business needs.

2.5 In-office conduct (desk hoteling, shared space etiquette, meeting room booking) follows Operations' facilities guidelines, referenced separately.

---

## 3. Company Communication Guidelines

3.1 **Tool-to-purpose mapping** (per Company Profile §12):
- **NexusConnect** — day-to-day, informal, channel-based team communication.
- **NexusMail** — formal, external, or documentation-required communication (contracts, client correspondence, official notices).
- **NexusDocs** — persistent documentation, decisions, and knowledge base entries.
- **The Signal** — official company-wide announcements from Executive leadership only; employees may not use this channel for department-level announcements.

3.2 Employees must respond to NexusConnect messages during Core Hours within a reasonable timeframe (target: same business day for non-urgent items).

3.3 Confidential, financial, HR, or legal matters must **never** be discussed in open NexusConnect channels; use private channels or NexusMail with appropriate distribution restrictions per **Security Policy** data classification rules.

3.4 External communications (press, public statements, social media on behalf of Nexus) require approval per the Approval Hierarchy (Marketing Director → CMO → CEO if material).

3.5 All company-wide written communication must reflect the Core Values (Clarity, Integrity, Ownership, Curiosity, Momentum) in tone and content.

---

## 4. Meeting Policy

4.1 Meetings should default to **NexusMeet** and be scheduled within Core Hours (10:00 AM – 3:00 PM local) to accommodate hybrid, remote, and cross-region attendees.

4.2 Every meeting invite must include a stated purpose and, where applicable, a NexusDocs agenda link.

4.3 **No-Meeting Guidance:** Recurring meetings without a documented agenda for two consecutive occurrences should be canceled or converted to an async NexusConnect/NexusDocs update.

4.4 Pod meetings (cross-functional) are coordinated by the Program Manager; department meetings are coordinated by the respective Director/VP.

4.5 Meeting recordings (via NexusMeet) containing confidential or client information must be stored and access-controlled per **Security Policy** data retention and access rules.

4.6 Large all-hands meetings are scheduled and communicated via **The Signal**.

---

## 5. Employee Responsibilities

5.1 Employees are responsible for understanding and adhering to all policies referenced in this document, including detailed **HR Policy**, **IT Policy**, **Finance Policy**, and **Security Policy** documents.

5.2 Employees must complete all mandatory training (security awareness, compliance, code of conduct) assigned via the NexusHR Portal within specified deadlines.

5.3 Employees must promptly report policy violations, safety concerns, or ethical concerns to their Manager, HR Business Partner, or through the confidential channel described in the Ethics section (§8).

5.4 Employees are accountable for the accuracy of time, expense, and PTO submissions per **Finance Policy** and **HR Policy** procedures.

5.5 Employees must safeguard company assets (devices, credentials, intellectual property) per **Security Policy** and **IT Policy** standards, and report lost/stolen equipment to IT Security immediately.

---

## 6. Workplace Expectations

6.1 All employees are expected to demonstrate professionalism and mutual respect in every interaction, in person, hybrid, or remote — consistent with the Nexus Way.

6.2 Harassment, discrimination, or retaliation of any kind is strictly prohibited. Reporting mechanisms and investigation procedures are detailed in the **HR Policy** (Anti-Harassment & Non-Discrimination section).

6.3 Dress code is "role-appropriate": business casual for client-facing roles/meetings, casual otherwise, unless a specific event dictates formal attire.

6.4 Attendance, punctuality, and availability during Core Hours are baseline expectations across all working models.

6.5 Performance is evaluated through the **Beacon Review** annual cycle, supplemented by ongoing Manager feedback; specifics are governed by the **HR Policy**.

---

## 7. Use of AI Tools

7.1 Employees may use Nexus-approved AI tools (including internal AI features within NexusFlow, NexusDocs, and any Nexus-sanctioned third-party AI assistants) to support productivity, subject to the restrictions below.

7.2 **Data protection:** No confidential client data, unreleased product information, financial data, or personal employee data may be input into any non-approved external AI tool. Approved tools and data-handling boundaries are defined jointly by **Security Policy** and **IT Policy**.

7.3 Employees must not rely on AI-generated output as final work product without human review — particularly for code (Engineering), legal/contractual language (Legal & Compliance), financial figures (Finance), or customer-facing communications (CS/Marketing).

7.4 Use of AI tools for hiring decisions, performance evaluations, or disciplinary actions is prohibited without HR and Legal oversight, to ensure fairness and compliance.

7.5 Any new AI tool requested for department use must go through the IT Security review and approval process before adoption (see **IT Policy** — Software Approval).

7.6 Employees must disclose material use of AI-generated content in client deliverables where contractually or ethically relevant, per **Legal & Compliance** guidance.

---

## 8. Ethics

8.1 Employees must act with integrity in all business dealings, avoiding conflicts of interest, bribery, or misuse of company position for personal gain.

8.2 Gifts, hospitality, or incentives from vendors/clients above a nominal value must be disclosed to Legal & Compliance.

8.3 Insider information (financial results, M&A activity, unreleased product features) must never be shared externally or used for personal trading advantage, consistent with **Finance Policy** and Legal & Compliance insider trading rules.

8.4 A confidential **Ethics & Integrity Line** (managed by Legal & Compliance, independent of direct management chains) is available for reporting suspected violations without fear of retaliation.

8.5 Ethics violations are investigated by Legal & Compliance in coordination with HR, and outcomes follow the disciplinary procedures defined in the **HR Policy**.

---

## 9. Sustainability

9.1 Nexus commits to reducing its operational carbon footprint through energy-efficient office operations (managed by Operations) and default-remote/hybrid work models that reduce commuting impact.

9.2 Offices are encouraged to minimize single-use materials; procurement of office supplies should favor sustainable vendors where feasible, subject to Finance's vendor approval process.

9.3 Cloud infrastructure decisions (Engineering & IT) should factor in energy-efficient hosting providers and data center regions where technically and financially viable.

9.4 Employees are encouraged to participate in optional annual sustainability initiatives coordinated by Operations and communicated via **The Signal**.

9.5 Business travel (see **Finance Policy** — Travel & Expense) should favor virtual meetings via NexusMeet where practical, reserving travel for high-value client or strategic engagements.

---

## 10. Business Continuity

10.1 Each department Director/VP is responsible for maintaining a Business Continuity Plan (BCP) covering critical role coverage, data access, and communication fallback procedures, coordinated by Operations.

10.2 In the event of a regional office disruption (natural disaster, outage, etc.), affected employees default to full remote work status until normal operations resume, communicated via **The Signal** and NexusConnect emergency channels.

10.3 IT maintains disaster recovery and data backup procedures for all critical systems (NexusFlow, NexusDocs, NexusBoard) per **Security Policy** and **IT Policy** standards, including defined Recovery Time Objectives (RTO) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPO).

10.4 Emergency contact trees are maintained by HR through the NexusHR Portal and must be kept current by all employees.

10.5 A cross-functional **Business Continuity Committee**, chaired by the COO with representation from IT, Security, HR, and Facilities, reviews and tests continuity plans at minimum annually.

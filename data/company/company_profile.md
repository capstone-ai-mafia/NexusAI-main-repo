# Nexus Technologies — Company Profile

## 1. Company Overview

**Nexus Technologies Inc.** ("Nexus," "the Company") is a mid-sized enterprise software company founded in 2013, specializing in cloud-based workflow automation, data integration platforms, and AI-assisted business tools. Nexus serves mid-market and enterprise clients across finance, healthcare, logistics, and retail sectors.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Founded | 2013 |
| Headquarters | Austin, Texas, USA |
| Employees | ~2,400 globally |
| Industry | Enterprise SaaS / Cloud Software |
| Flagship Product | **NexusFlow** — a workflow automation and integration platform |
| Ownership | Privately held, venture-backed (Series D) |
| Fiscal Year | January – December |

## 5. Organizational Structure

Nexus operates on a **hybrid matrix structure**: functional departments (Engineering, Product, Sales, etc.) report vertically through department leadership, while cross-functional initiatives ("Pods") are coordinated horizontally through Program Managers.

```
                        Board of Directors
                              │
                    Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
                              │
   ┌───────────┬───────────┬──────────┼───────────┬───────────┬───────────┐
  CTO         CPO         CFO        COO         CHRO        CMO         CRO
   │           │           │          │            │           │           │
Engineering  Product   Finance &   Operations    People &   Marketing    Revenue
& IT Dept    Dept      Accounting  Dept          Culture    Dept         (Sales &
                        Dept                      Dept                    CS) Dept
```

**C-Suite Roles:**
- **CEO** — Chief Executive Officer
- **CTO** — Chief Technology Officer
- **CPO** — Chief Product Officer
- **CFO** — Chief Financial Officer
- **COO** — Chief Operating Officer
- **CHRO** — Chief Human Resources Officer
- **CMO** — Chief Marketing Officer
- **CRO** — Chief Revenue Officer

### 7.1 Leveling Framework (L1-L7)

Nexus uses a single company-wide leveling scale, applied equally to the **IC (Individual Contributor) track** and the **Management track**. Levels map onto the role ladders above (L1-L2 approximate entry/associate roles, L3-L4 approximate Manager/Senior IC roles, L5-L6 approximate Director/Staff roles, L7 approximates VP-level IC or management roles), with the **ELT** (Executive Leadership Team, i.e., the C-Suite collectively, §5) sitting above L7. Compensation, promotion, and expense-policy tiers referenced elsewhere (e.g., corporate card limits, airfare class) use this **L1-L7** scale.

### 7.2 Squads, Pods, and DRIs

- **Squad** is used interchangeably with **Pod** (§13 Glossary) to describe a cross-functional team assembled around a product initiative or customer segment.
- **DRI (Directly Responsible Individual)** is the single named person accountable for a specific work item, decision, or deliverable at a given time, ensuring nothing is left without an owner. Before extended leave or offboarding, employees complete a **DRI handoff/handover** to a Squad/Pod teammate.

## 8. Reporting Hierarchy

Standard reporting flows **bottom-up** through direct managers to department VPs/Directors, then to the respective C-Suite executive, then to the CEO, who reports to the **Board of Directors**.

```
Individual Contributor → Team Lead/Manager → Director/VP → C-Suite Executive → CEO → Board
```

**Cross-functional Pods:** For product initiatives, a **Program Manager** coordinates representatives from Engineering, Product, Marketing, and CS. Pod members retain a "dotted line" reporting relationship to the Program Manager while their "solid line" reporting remains within their home department.

## 9. Approval Hierarchy

### 9.1 General Principles

- **No self-approval:** the person requesting an approval (a purchase, a promotion, a contract) may never be its sole approver. A second, more senior approver is always required.
- **Escalation by materiality:** approval authority scales with financial, legal, or reputational exposure — larger or riskier decisions require more senior sign-off, per the thresholds in §9.2-§9.5.
- Detailed policy documents (HR, Finance, Legal & Compliance, Security) implement these principles with the specific thresholds and workflows below, and must remain consistent with them.

### 9.2 Hiring & Promotion Approval

| Approval Type | Threshold | Approver(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Hiring requisition | Any role | Hiring Manager → HR Director → CHRO |
| Promotion | Any level | Reporting Manager (sponsor) → Calibration Panel → Director/VP sign-off; VP-and-above promotions escalate to the CHRO |

### 9.3 Purchase, Expense & Procurement Approval

| Approval Type | Threshold | Approver(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Purchase requisition | Under $1,000 | Direct Manager |
| Purchase requisition | $1,000 – $10,000 | Department Director/VP |
| Purchase requisition | Above $10,000 | CFO + relevant C-Suite sponsor |
| New vendor/contract | Any | Legal & Compliance review → Department VP → CFO (if financial) |
| Expense reimbursement | Under $500 | Direct Manager |
| Expense reimbursement | Above $500 | Direct Manager + Finance |

### 9.4 Document & Policy Sign-off Authority

| Document Type | Required Sign-off |
|---|---|
| Company-wide policy (HR, Legal, Security, IT, Finance manuals) | Executive Leadership Team (ELT — the C-Suite collectively, §5) |
| Security / compliance policy | CISO + Legal & Compliance (General Counsel) |
| Customer-facing legal / contract terms | Legal & Compliance (General Counsel) + CFO (or CRO for commercial terms) |

### 9.5 Commercial (Customer) Contract & Other Approvals

| Approval Type | Threshold | Approver(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Product feature launch | Major release | Product Manager → VP of Product → CPO |
| PTO / Leave request | Standard | Direct Manager |
| External public statement/press | Any | Marketing Director → CMO → CEO (if material) |
| Commercial contract discount | Up to 15% | Sales Manager |
| Commercial contract discount | 15–30% | Regional Sales Director |
| Commercial contract | Above 30% discount or non-standard terms | VP of Sales + CFO |
| Enterprise commercial deal | Above $1M ARR | CRO + CEO |

*(Detailed policy documents governing these thresholds are issued separately and must remain consistent with this hierarchy.)*

## 11. Working Model

Nexus follows a **Hybrid-Flexible Working Model**:
- **Hybrid Employees:** 3 days/week in-office (Tue–Thu "Core Collaboration Days"), 2 days remote.
- **Remote-First Roles:** Fully remote, with quarterly in-person "Nexus Sync" gatherings at the nearest regional office.
- **Core Hours:** 10:00 AM – 3:00 PM (local time zone) for meeting availability.
- **Flexible Hours:** Outside core hours, employees manage their own schedule to meet deliverables.
